Educational Summary

Silicon is the quintessential element of semiconductors. Having 4 valence electrons allows it to build highly stable covalent bonds with 4 neighbors in a crystal lattice structure.

High heat breaks covalent bonds, creating spontaneous electron-hole pairs.

0.0 V (None)

Free electrons drift to positive terminal. Holes migrate slowly to negative terminal via valence jumps.

Substituted Atomic Physics

The lattice is purely intrinsic. Every Silicon atom has a net +4 core charge perfectly sharing its four electrons in stable covalent bonds with 4 neighbors. No free carriers are present except those generated by heat.
